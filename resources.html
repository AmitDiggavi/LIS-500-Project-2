Implicit Bias & Intersectionality Resource Page

Our goal is to make the concepts of implicit bias and intersectionality
easy to understand for first-year college students,
offering clear explanations and helpful resources.

Bias Overview
Implicit bias refers to the subconscious attitudes or stereotypes we hold that influence our actions and decisions without us realizing it. For example, biases can show up in hiring practices, classroom interactions, or social settings. To explore your own potential biases, we recommend taking the Implicit Bias Test (IBT), which helps uncover hidden prejudices.

Intersectionality Overview
Intersectionality explains how different aspects of our identity—like race, gender, and sexuality—interact to shape our unique experiences. For example, someone might face both racial and gender biases, which combine to create specific challenges. In college, understanding intersectionality is key to navigating diverse environments and being aware of how identity influences experience.

Connecting Implicit Bias & Intersectionality
Implicit biases are often influenced by our intersecting identities, leading to more complex forms of discrimination. Recognizing these connections is essential in fostering more inclusive social interactions, institutional policies, and even tech use.

  • Intersectionality Explained - An educational video explaining intersectionality and its role in social justice. Link to YouTube video
  • Suggested Reading: “The Urgency of Intersectionality” by Kimberlé Crenshaw - A must-read article that explains how multiple forms of bias affect people and why intersectionality matters in social justice efforts.
  • “Blindspot: Hidden Biases of Good People” by Mahzarin R. Banaji and Anthony G. Greenwald - This book explores how implicit biases form and influence behavior, using research and personal stories to engage readers.
