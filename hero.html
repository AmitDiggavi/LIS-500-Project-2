Tech Hero

Tim Berners-Lee

Who is Tim Berners-Lee?
Tim Berners-Lee, born in 1955, is the creator of the World Wide Web—a groundbreaking invention that forever changed how we communicate and access information.
A computer scientist from the UK, he built the web in 1989 while working at CERN.
Lesser-known, but just as impressive, is his dedication to keeping the web open and free for all, not just big corporations.

Why Tim Berners-Lee?
We chose Tim because of his lifelong commitment to equity and inclusion.
His vision for the web was one of universal access—anyone, anywhere, should be able to connect and share ideas without barriers.
He continues to fight for digital rights, making sure the internet remains a place for everyone.

Contributions to Equity & Inclusion
Berners-Lee is a strong advocate for net neutrality and has worked tirelessly to bridge the digital divide through his World Wide Web Foundation.
The foundation promotes open access, fights censorship, and ensures that underrepresented communities can harness the web's power to amplify their voices and access crucial resources.
The internet he built has become a platform for social activism, leveling the playing field for marginalized groups.

Relevance to Implicit Bias & Intersectionality
By championing an open web, Tim Berners-Lee has helped reduce barriers for individuals from all backgrounds,
creating opportunities for intersectional communities to connect, share their experiences, and challenge biases.
His work ensures that the web remains a powerful tool for raising awareness about issues like implicit bias and systemic discrimination.
